Dianna Offor, (1999)

Dianna-Maria Somtochukwu Offor-Ugoji (b.1999) is an abstract artist and expressionist painter. She grew up drawing and sketching as early as the age of 5. In highschool, she completed a drawing assignment for her classmates which led to the discovery of her talents. She went on to master watercolor and poster colors during her early years. She later studied fine arts at the University of Lagos graduating with a BA in Creative Arts (textile design) in 2021. It was also where she expanded into a new range of materials for painting including pastel, oil and acrylic. ... She is known as ‘God’s Artist’ because of the ‘Christian’ abstract imagery depicted in her paintings and her strong belief in God. Her style of art is considered “Biblical” as it reflects on messages from the bible and christianity. Using bright vibrant colors, layers, texture and contrast, she highlights abstract color blocks with simple use of contrasting colors and chalk pastels.
She has taken part in group exhibitions including Femme Fatale at Lagoon Gallery (2018), Purple Place at Olori Gallery (2022) and Grow up at the Art Hotel (2022).
